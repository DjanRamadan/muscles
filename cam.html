First Generation (1967-1969)

1967 Camaro SS

Featured a 350 cubic inch V8 engine producing 295 horsepower. Known for its powerful engine options and iconic design with hidden headlights.

1969 Camaro Z/28

Equipped with a 302 cubic inch V8 engine producing 290 horsepower. Designed for Trans-Am racing with a focus on handling and performance.

1969 Camaro COPO 427

Came with a 427 cubic inch V8 engine producing 425 horsepower. Built for drag racing, it had minimal luxury features and maximum power.

Second Generation (1970-1981)

1970 Camaro SS

Powered by a 396 cubic inch V8 engine producing 375 horsepower. Featured a split bumper design and improved handling over the first generation.

1973 Camaro Z/28

Included a 350 cubic inch V8 engine producing 245 horsepower. Known for its aggressive styling and performance, even as emissions regulations began to impact horsepower.

1977 Camaro Z/28

Had a 350 cubic inch V8 engine producing 185 horsepower. Marked the return of the Z/28 after a brief hiatus, focusing on handling and style.

Third Generation (1982-1992)

1985 Camaro IROC-Z

Featured a 305 cubic inch V8 engine producing 215 horsepower. Named after the International Race of Champions, it had a focus on performance and handling.

1987 Camaro Z/28

Came with a 350 cubic inch V8 engine producing 225 horsepower. Known for its aerodynamic design and improved fuel injection systems.

1992 Camaro Z/28

Included a 350 cubic inch V8 engine producing 245 horsepower. Marked the end of the third generation with a blend of classic style and modern performance.

Fourth Generation (1993-2002)

1996 Camaro Z/28 SS

Included a 350 cubic inch LT1 V8 engine producing 305 horsepower. Offered a blend of performance and comfort with improved suspension and handling.

1998 Camaro Z/28

Powered by a 346 cubic inch LS1 V8 engine producing 305 horsepower. Introduced the LS1 engine, significantly boosting performance and efficiency.

2002 Camaro SS

Featured a 346 cubic inch LS1 V8 engine producing 325 horsepower. The final model of the fourth generation, known for its performance and advanced features.

Fifth Generation (2010-2015)

2010 Camaro SS

Came with a 376 cubic inch LS3 V8 engine producing 426 horsepower. Marked the return of the Camaro with a retro-inspired design and modern technology.

2012 Camaro ZL1

Featured a 376 cubic inch supercharged LSA V8 engine producing 580 horsepower. Known for its incredible performance and track-ready capabilities.

2014 Camaro Z/28

Powered by a 427 cubic inch LS7 V8 engine producing 505 horsepower. Designed for track use with advanced aerodynamics and performance enhancements.

Sixth Generation (2016-present)

2016 Camaro SS

Included a 376 cubic inch LT1 V8 engine producing 455 horsepower. Featured a refined design, improved performance, and modern technology.

2018 Camaro ZL1 1LE

Equipped with a 376 cubic inch supercharged LT4 V8 engine producing 650 horsepower. Designed for maximum track performance with advanced suspension and aerodynamics.

2020 Camaro LT1

Came with a 376 cubic inch LT1 V8 engine producing 455 horsepower. Offered a blend of performance and value with a focus on driving enjoyment.
